Cornell Formula Racing SAE

I'm on the electrical team of Cornell FSAE, helping to design and build the electrical systems on the a formula style racecar each year.

Since our transition to electric, I helped design the low-voltage fusebox for the car. I learned a lot about PCB design, solid state switching, and soldering!

Low-voltage fusebox

For the LV fusebox, I iterated on the design from the previous year. Because we switched to electric vehicle, I had to redesign the fusebox for different requirements. For the design, I chose to use n-channel MOSFETs with a gate driver chip for switching for lower onstate resistance, as there were failures due to overheating in previous iterations.

MathWorks

EDG Intern - Software Engineering

During my time at MathWorks, I worked primarily on two projects. I loved my time there as an intern; the company culture is amazingly friendly and enthusiastic where the work is also impactful and specialized.

From June to August of 2019, I worked on the following projects:

USB over network hardware test infrastructure

I worked across multiple teams to set up internal infrastructure for connecting hardware boards over the network. This improved the testing framework by reducing overhead and inefficiencies due to physical board testing limitations. Furthermore, the new testing framework extended hardware board testing to any user on the network, enabling hardware testing anywhere accessible by network.

Custom board support framework

I worked with QE engineers to rebuild the custom board support framework API, leveraging objected-oriented design to help QE engineers build software objects for hardware testing. The new framework reduced test writing time drastically, enabling 2 years of tests to be written in a week.

White Fox Scooters

Hardware Engineer

Over the winter of 2018-2019, I helped to install GPS tracking devices in 12 ride-share electric scooters.
